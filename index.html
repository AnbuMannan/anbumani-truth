Understanding the Case

What CBI Claimed

Case Active — Delhi High Court — CRL.M.C. 4442/2015 & 4443/2015 — Last heard: Jan 6, 2026
The Core Allegation

Illegal Permission to Index Medical College, Indore

CBI alleged that as Union Health Minister (2004–2009), Dr. Anbumani approved MBBS admissions for Index Medical College Hospital & Research Centre (IMCHRC), Indore for 2008–09 — despite the Medical Council of India (MCI) recommending against it due to inadequate faculty and clinical resources.

The Second Case

Rohilkhand Medical College, Bareilly, UP

A parallel CBI case alleged similar favours to Rohilkhand Medical College and Hospital (RMCH) in Bareilly — involving renewal of MBBS admission approvals for 2008–09 despite MCI objections.

Sections Charged

Criminal Conspiracy & Corruption

IPC Sections 120-B (Criminal Conspiracy), 420 (Cheating), 465 & 468 (Forgery), 471 (Using forged documents). Prevention of Corruption Act Sections 13(1)(d) and 13(2) — abusing official position for pecuniary advantage.

Critical Point

Financial Gain to College — Not to Anbumani

The CBI's own chargesheet identifies the "pecuniary advantage" as going to the college — not to Dr. Anbumani personally. No direct bribe or financial gain to Anbumani has ever been alleged or evidenced.

"The petitioner is falsely implicated in the present case; he was competent to grant permission on the basis of the note put and signed by the recommending authority and there is no evidence to connect the petitioner in commission of conspiracy."— Sr. Advocate Mohit Gupta, Counsel for Dr. Anbumani Ramadoss, Delhi HC 2019

The Central Mystery

The Hidden Video

MCI Inspection Videography — October 20, 2008

A central inspection team visited Index Medical College on October 20, 2008 and videographed their inspection. This team found adequate facilities and gave a clean report. CBI seized the video — but deliberately excluded it from the chargesheet.

✦ Anbumani's Position

The video shows the college had no deficiencies — vindicating the permission granted.
The ASG stated before the Supreme Court that the inspection was videographed and "no deficiency was found".
Suppressing the video violates fair trial rights. The Delhi HC called it "material evidence" in 2019.

✦ CBI's Silence

CBI did not dispute the video exists — confirmed in court.
CBI chose not to include it in the chargesheet — but never explained why.
If the video shows the college was compliant, it would directly contradict CBI's case.
Legal Defence Arguments

5 Pillars of Innocence

01

Ministerial Process Followed

Dr. Anbumani granted permission based on a unanimous file note signed by the recommending authority — standard ministerial procedure. A minister acting on officers' advice cannot be criminally liable simply because the decision was wrong.

02

Final Inspection Cleared the College

The final permission came after the October 20, 2008 central team — the most recent inspection — found adequate facilities. The ASG confirmed this before the Supreme Court. Anbumani acted on the latest authoritative report.

03

No Personal Financial Gain

Not a single rupee of personal bribe or gain has ever been alleged against Dr. Anbumani in either chargesheet. The "pecuniary advantage" flows to the college — not the minister. No money trail, no bank transaction, nothing.

04

Meeting Alone ≠ Conspiracy

CBI points to the college chairman being in the Ministry on the day permission was signed. But courts have held — citing Supreme Court precedent — that criminal conspiracy cannot be inferred merely from two parties meeting.

05

CBI Withheld Material Evidence

CBI seized the inspection videography but excluded it from the chargesheet — a fundamental breach. The Delhi HC in 2019 explicitly ruled it is "material evidence" that must be given to accused. This ruling signals the court's own scepticism.
